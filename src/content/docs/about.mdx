I'm Michael Borck. I build things, teach people, and try to figure out how AI can be genuinely useful in education without requiring a data centre or giving up your privacy.

## What I do

I'm based at **Curtin University** in Perth, Western Australia, where I teach and lead [LocoLabo](https://locolabo.org) — an applied AI research lab that runs entirely on secondhand consumer hardware. The lab's premise is simple: most people don't have access to frontier compute, so we study what's actually possible on the hardware they do have.

I also build [privacy-first educational tools](https://borck.education) — desktop apps, CLI tools, and teaching kits that run locally. No cloud dependency, no data leaving the device. Tools like AI tutors, conversation practice, curriculum generators, and grading assistants.

## The other stuff

Outside of work, I run [RetroVerse Studios](https://retroverse.studio), a small indie game studio making retro-styled games with modern mechanics.

I'm an amateur astronomer at [The Little Perth Observatory](https://thelittleperthobservatory.com) — five homemade observing stations, a philosophy of "skills beat gear", and a lot of time under dark skies.

I write occasionally at [The 80-20 Workshop](https://8020workshop.com) about building, making, and the 80/20 principle applied to learning.
