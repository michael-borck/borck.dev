I lead [LocoLabo](https://locolabo.org), an applied AI research initiative at the School of Marketing and Management, Curtin University. Our work sits at the intersection of technology, education, and human-computer interaction.

<div class="philosophy-quote">
"We map the floor because most people live there, and nobody is documenting it honestly."
</div>

The lab studies what local AI can accomplish on modest consumer hardware — secondhand machines totalling under $1,000 AUD — while prioritising privacy-first approaches for educational contexts.

## Projects

<div class="research-grid">

<div class="research-card">

### LocoLLM

A routed network of fine-tuned specialist models using LoRA adapters, designed to run on consumer hardware without cloud infrastructure. Student capstone projects contribute to the research.

<a href="https://locollm.org">locollm.org</a>

</div>

<div class="research-card">

### LocoBench

Systematic performance testing across consumer GPU memory tiers. Establishing honest baseline expectations rather than peak capabilities.

<a href="https://locobench.org">locobench.org</a>

</div>

<div class="research-card">

### LocoConvoy

Multi-GPU parallelism optimised for standard PCIe connections. Load balancing and speculative decoding without specialised high-bandwidth interconnects.

<a href="https://lococonvoy.org">lococonvoy.org</a>

</div>

<div class="research-card">

### LocoEnsayo

Simulated professional environments where students practise realistic scenarios before high-stakes situations. A rehearsal tool for learning.

<a href="https://locoensayo.org">locoensayo.org</a>

</div>

</div>

## Themes

My broader research interests span:

- **AI in Education** — How local, privacy-first AI tools change the teaching and learning dynamic
- **Human-Computer Interaction** — How people interact with AI systems, especially when constraints (speed, model size) shape the experience
- **Constraint-Driven Innovation** — What emerges when you take away the cloud, the budget, and the assumptions
